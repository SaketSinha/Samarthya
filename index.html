The Crisis

The Crisis

As per a UDISE report only 9% of the schools are RTE compliant. 92% of parent members that we work with are unaware of the privileges’ under RTE. 44% boys and 43% girls passed 8th grade with less than 60% marks in 2015- 16. Section 21 of the Right To Education (RTE) Act (2009), which mandates the formation of School Management Committees (SMCs) in all government-run or aided schools. SMCs are responsible for monitoring school functioning and finances, with parents/guardians making up 75% of the committee.


In a school in east Delhi, there’re 20 classrooms and around 2000 boys. Each class has only 30 benches, which means around 60 children sit on the floor. Out of the 6 fans in the classroom, only 3 work. There is no water supply in the evening. Teachers are frequently not present in the class. The absence of teachers from the classrooms, the nonworking fans, 100 students in each class are either violations of RTE or government rules.


One of the major pain-points in the implementation of programs to improve school quality has been the inability of the system to fix accountability at the last mile. The mode of fixing accountability from the top creates extreme challenges. In such a scenario, empowering communities at a school-level for governance is critical to the success of the movement towards quality. This is what Samarthya is working towards through parent members of SMCs.

Theory of Change

"We provide knowledge and skills about RTE to parents of public (government) school going children. Our customer has a problem that there children are not learning. We solve this problem by informing and equipping SMC parent members with the knowledge and skills required to perform their duties as envisioned in Section 21 of RTE. We’re unique because we foster parent leaders {resource person(s) who knows RTE, government systems in and out} from within a community to make community led implementation of RTE a reality."
